üìã Understanding US Income Tax

The United States uses a progressive tax system where higher income is taxed at higher rates. Your income is divided into brackets, with each portion taxed at its corresponding rate. This means everyone pays the same rate on the same portions of income.

For 2026, federal tax brackets range from 10% to 37%. However, your effective tax rate (total tax divided by income) is typically much lower than your top bracket due to this progressive structure.

What's the difference between marginal and effective tax rate?‚ñº
Marginal rate is the tax on your next dollar of income (your highest bracket). Effective rate is your total tax divided by total income. Most people pay an effective rate 5-10% lower than their marginal rate due to the progressive system.
Does this include state and local taxes?‚ñº
State tax is optional in this calculator. Select your approximate state rate. Local taxes vary widely and aren't included. Some states (TX, FL, WA, NV) have no income tax.
