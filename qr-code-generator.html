üìã What are QR Codes?

QR (Quick Response) codes are two-dimensional barcodes that can be scanned by smartphones to quickly access information. Invented in 1994 by Denso Wave for tracking automotive parts, they're now used worldwide for payments, marketing, authentication, and information sharing.

Unlike traditional barcodes, QR codes can store much more data - up to 7,089 numeric characters or 4,296 alphanumeric characters. They're also readable from any angle and include error correction, meaning they can still be scanned even if partially damaged.
