üí° The Rule of 72

Divide 72 by the inflation rate to estimate how long it takes prices to double. At 3% inflation, prices double in about 24 years.

üìä Historical Average

US inflation has averaged about 3% annually over the long term. Recent years saw higher rates (7-9% in 2021-2022).

üéØ Beating Inflation

To maintain purchasing power, investments must return more than inflation. Stocks historically average 7% after inflation.

üìã Understanding Inflation

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, causing purchasing power to fall. As inflation increases, every dollar you own buys a smaller percentage of goods or services.

The most common measure of inflation is the Consumer Price Index (CPI), which tracks the prices of a basket of goods and services over time. Central banks typically target 2% annual inflation as a healthy rate for economic growth.

What causes inflation?‚ñº
Inflation is caused by increased money supply, rising production costs, supply shortages, or increased demand. The COVID-19 pandemic and subsequent supply chain issues caused recent spikes in inflation globally.
How can I protect against inflation?‚ñº
Invest in assets that historically outpace inflation: stocks, real estate, Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS), and commodities. Avoid holding large amounts of cash for long periods.
